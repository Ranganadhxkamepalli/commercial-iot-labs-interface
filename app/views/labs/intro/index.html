Read the Objectives

Get off to a good start by making sure your computer, Intel® Edison, and Intel® IoT Gateway are ready for IoT development.

By the end of this module, you should be able to:

  • Connect to your Intel® IoT Gateway from your development computer
  • Unbox and setup your Intel® Edison Hardware
  • Connect to your Intel® Edison from your development computer using a serial connection.
  • Connect your Intel® Edison to your Intel® IoT Gateway's hotspot
  • Deploy an application to your Intel® Edison using the Intel® XDK

Find your Gateway's MAC address

Establish an SSH connection and login to your Intel® IoT Gateway using PuTTY.

Check the side of your Intel® IoT Gateway. There is a tab located just next to the power jack. Pull on the tab to expose the gateway's product numbers and MAC address.

The last four characters of the WIRELESS MAC address will be used in the gatway's network SSID

Connect to your Gateway's Wi-Fi Hotspot SSID

From your development computer, connect to your Gateway's network hotspot. It will start with the letters IDPDK and then end with the last four characters of your MAC address.

The default password to your Wi-Fi network is windriveridp

Download and connect to your Gateway with a terminal client (PuTTY)

Windows does not come with a built-in terminal emulator so download and use the PuTTY client.

  1. Visit the PuTTY download page.
  2. Under the "For Windows on Intel x86" heading, click on the "putty.exe" link to download the latest release version to your computer.
  3. Double-click putty.exe on your computer to launch PuTTY.

    The IP address of the Intel IoT Gateway is 192.168.1.1.

  4. You can login with the username root and the password root.

You should now be logged into the Intel® IoT Gateway.

Insert the Intel Edison onto the Arduino expansion board

Place the Intel® Edison module within the white outline on the Arduino expansion board, lining up the holes on the Intel® Edison module with the screws on the expansion board.

Be sure that you have pressed the Intel Edison down firmly and felt a click.

Press down on the Intel® Edison module just below the words "What will you make?" until you feel a snap.

Caution: Unless you make sure your board is seated properly, it may not work or turn on at all. When you turn the attached module and expansion board on their side, both pieces should fit evenly and sit in parallel with each other.

Secure the Intel Edison to the Arduino board with the mini hex bolts

Use the two hex nuts to secure the module to the expansion board. Hand tighten (but not too tight).

Install the plastic spacers

To add stability to the expansion board and to help avoid accidental short circuits, attach the four plastic spacers supplied in your Intel® Edison box to act as "legs" for the expansion board.

  1. In one of the four holes in the corner of the Arduino expansion board, insert a screw through the top.
  2. Twist and tighten one of the white plastic spacers onto the screw. The spacer should be on the bottom of the expansion board to act as legs.
  3. Repeat for the remaining three screws and spacers.

Connect the cables between the Intel Edison and your development computer and boot the Intel Edison

Connect your development computer to the Intel® Edison using the supplied micro-USB cables.

Connect your Intel® Edison to your computer as described below.

  1. (1) microswitch in "USB device mode" (i.e. toggled down towards the micro-USB ports)
  2. (2) device mode micro-USB cable plugged into your computer
  3. (3) UART/serial micro-USB cable plugged into your computer
  4. (4) External power supply connected plugged in the power outlet
  5. The Arduino expansion board for Intel® Edison can be powered via the DC power jack, the device mode micro-USB port, or both. Since we will be using actuators that demand more power from the board than the USB is likely to provide, we will be using the external supply.

Be sure the green light is on

When your computer is plugged in you will see a green light. Some laptops do not output sufficient power for the Intel Edison. In cases where there is not enough power, the Intel Edison board may start and then reboot in a loop.

Intel Edison has booted

Wait one minute for the Intel® Edison to finish booting up.

How do you know when the board is ready?

You will know that the Intel® Edison is fully initialized when your computer mounts a new drive (much like inserting a SD card into your computer).

Download the Intel® Edison Software Development Tools

Go to the software download page for the Intel® Edison. The developer tools are available for Windows, MacOS and Ubuntu. Select the download that corresponds to your computer.

Double-click on the installer file on your computer to begin installation.

Start the installation process.

Select the Intel® Edison drivers, flash the latest firmware and install the Intel® XDK development environment.

The default settings can be adjusted to your needs, or leave the defaults as is.

In the component selection screen, check the options you would like to install:

  • Drivers: Installs the Intel® Edison drivers and FTDI serial drivers for Windows.
  • Update image: Flashes the latest firmware image directly on your Intel® Edison. (Note: You will not be able to use this option until after you have installed the drivers. Re-run the integrated installer if you wish to use it to flash Intel® Edison firmware.)

  • You have the option to install several development environments. In this workshop, we will be using the Intel XDK. Feel free to install the others though, if you wish.

    • When done, click “Finish” to close the installation wizard.

    If there are any errors during installation, you will need to resolve them manually.

Flash Intel Edison with Latest Image

Continue to Flash latest firmware on Intel Edison. Updating firmware on Intel Edison might take up to 10 minutes.

Set a Device Password and Name

Continue to the Password Set and SSH section of the installer. Set a hostname for your Intel Edison.

Set a Device Password and Name

Continue to the Password Set and SSH section of the installer. Set a password for your Intel Edison.

Connect the Intel Edison to your Intel IoT Gateway WiFi

Connect the Intel Edison to the WiFi hotspot from your Intel IoT Gateway.

Remember that you can find the SSID that your Intel IoT Gateway is broadcasting by looking at the MAC address of your Gateway.

Get off to a good start by making sure your computer, Intel® Edison, and Intel® IoT Gateway are ready for IoT development.

By the end of this module, you should be able to:

  • Connect to your Intel® IoT Gateway from your development computer
  • Unbox and setup your Intel® Edison Hardware
  • Connect to your Intel® Edison from your development computer using a serial connection.
  • Connect your Intel® Edison to your Intel® IoT Gateway's hotspot
  • Deploy an application to your Intel® Edison using the Intel® XDK

Establish an SSH connection and login to your Intel® IoT Gateway using PuTTY.

Check the side of your Intel® IoT Gateway. There is a tab located just next to the power jack. Pull on the tab to expose the gateway's product numbers and MAC address.

The last four characters of the MAC address will be used in the gatway's network SSID

From your development computer, connect to your Gateway's network hotspot. It will start with the letters IDPDK and then end with the last four characters of your MAC address.

The default password to your Wi-Fi network is windriveridp

Windows does not come with a built-in terminal emulator so download and use the PuTTY client.

  1. Visit the PuTTY download page.
  2. Under the "For Windows on Intel x86" heading, click on the "putty.exe" link to download the latest release version to your computer.
  3. Double-click putty.exe on your computer to launch PuTTY.

    The IP address of the Intel IoT Gateway is 192.168.1.1.

  4. You can login with the username root and the password root.

You should now be logged into the Intel® IoT Gateway.

The Intel® Edison module is meant to be used with breakout boards or custom PCBs. You will need to secure your Intel® Edison module to the Arduino expansion board before you can use it.

  1. Place the Intel® Edison module within the white outline on the Arduino expansion board, lining up the holes on the Intel® Edison module with the screws on the expansion board.

  2. Press down on the Intel® Edison module just below the words "What will you make?" until you feel a snap.

  3. Use the two hex nuts to secure the module to the expansion board. Hand tighten (but not too tight) the hex nuts onto the two screws that protrude through the board.

To add stability to the expansion board and to help avoid accidental short circuits, attach the four plastic spacers supplied in your Intel® Edison box to act as "legs" for the expansion board.

  1. In one of the four holes in the corner of the Arduino expansion board, insert a screw through the top.
  2. Twist and tighten one of the white plastic spacers onto the screw. The spacer should be on the bottom of the expansion board to act as legs.
  3. Repeat for the remaining three screws and spacers.

Connect your development computer to the Intel® Edison using the supplied micro-USB cables.

  1. Connect your Intel® Edison to your computer as described below.

    1. (1) microswitch in "USB device mode" (i.e. toggled down towards the micro-USB ports)
    2. (2) device mode micro-USB cable plugged into your computer
    3. (3) UART/serial micro-USB cable plugged into your computer
    4. The Arduino expansion board for Intel® Edison can be powered via the DC power jack, the device mode micro-USB port, or both. This setup uses only a micro-USB cable for power.

  2. When your computer is plugged in you will see a green light.

  3. Wait one minute for the Intel® Edison to finish booting up.

    How do you know when the board is ready?

    You will know that the Intel® Edison is fully initialized when your computer mounts a new drive (much like inserting a SD card into your computer).

Go to the software download page for the Intel® Edison. The developer tools are available for Windows, MacOS and Ubuntu. Select the download that corresponds to your computer.

Start the installation process.

Is the "Next" button greyed out?

The integrated installer is unable to detect your Intel® Edison. Ensure that the board is powered on and plugged into your computer via the device mode micro-USB port on the expansion board. Otherwise you will need to install the Windows drivers manually. Refer to Set Up Your Computer - Windows (manual).

The default settings can be adjusted to your needs, or leave the defaults as is.

In the component selection screen, check the options you would like to install:

  • Drivers: Installs the Intel® Edison drivers and FTDI serial drivers for Windows.
  • Update image: Flashes the latest firmware image directly on your Intel® Edison. (Note: You will not be able to use this option until after you have installed the drivers. Re-run the integrated installer if you wish to use it to flash Intel® Edison firmware.)

  • You have the option to install several development environments. In this workshop, we will be using the Intel XDK.

    • When done, click “Finish” to close the installation wizard.

    If there are any errors during installation, you will need to resolve them manually.

To ensure driver installation changes take effect, reboot your Windows computer at this point.

Based on your version of Windows, select the option to fully shut down and restart your computer.

Windows 7 and below:

  1. Open Start menu.
  2. Type “device manager” in the search field.
  3. Select "Device Manager" under "Control Panel"

Windows 8:

  1. Go into Windows Desktop mode.
  2. Right-click on the Start menu button.
  3. Select "Device Manager".

In Device Manager, locate and toggle open Ports (COM & LPT).

The Intel® Edison drivers for Windows include several USB drivers in one installer package. These drivers enable important features, such as:

  • Composite Device Class (CDC) for programming the board via the Arduino IDE,
  • Remote Network Driver Interface Spec (RNDIS) for Ethernet over USB, and
  • Device Firmware Upgrade (DFU) for updating firmware on devices.

Connect a micro-USB cable to the device mode micro-USB port of the Intel® Edison expansion board, and the other end to your computer.

If you see both "Intel Edison USB Composite Device" and "Intel Edison Virtual Com Port" items show up in Device Manager under “Ports (COM & LPT)”, the drivers have been successfully installed.

FTDI CDM drivers allow your computer to communicate with USB serial devices, including the Intel® Edison.

Power the Intel® Edison via the device mode micro-USB port and/or via the power barrel connector.

Connect a micro-USB cable to the serial micro-USB port of the Intel® Edison and the other end to your computer.

If you see "USB Serial Port" show up in Device Manager under “Ports (COM & LPT)”, the drivers have been successfully installed.

This setup document will guide you through establishing a serial connection to either the Intel® Galileo or the Intel® Edison using a Windows computer.

If you need to configure your IoT board, you will need to remotely connect to the Intel® Edison or Intel® Galileo. Once connected to your Intel® IoT board, you have access to the Linux-based Yocto operating system running on the board.

You can then execute special Linux commands such:

  1. changing the hostname and password,
  2. setting up Wi-Fi, or
  3. flashing new firmware.

  1. Set up your board using the configure_edison command with the --setup flag.

    You will use these credentials to connect to the board if you need to re-connect the serial connection, or program the board using the Intel® XDK in the next section of this workshop module.

  2. Follow the prompts to set the device password to be "intel123".

    To confirm your entry, type "Y" and press Enter.
